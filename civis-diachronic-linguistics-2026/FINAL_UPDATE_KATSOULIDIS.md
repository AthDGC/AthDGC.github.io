# Final Update: Katsoulidis Font Implementation

## Latest Change: Authentic Greek Typography

The website now uses **Katsoulidis** - a prestigious Greek typeface designed by Takis Katsoulidis specifically for academic Greek publishing.

## Font Configuration

### Katsoulidis (Headings & Emphasis)
- **Designer:** Takis Katsoulidis (renowned Greek type designer)
- **Origin:** Created 1986-1989 as part of Greek university research programs
- **Foundry:** Fonts.GR (Greek Digital Type Library)
- **Style:** Modern evolution of Didot with enhanced curves and improved legibility
- **Usage:** All headings, titles, section headers
- **Why this font:** 
  - Designed in Greek universities for academic publishing
  - Created by Greece's most respected type designer
  - Perfect for University of Athens identity
  - Represents the highest standard of modern Greek typography

### Roboto (Body Text)
- **Designer:** Christian Robertson (Google)
- **Usage:** Body text, navigation, interface
- **Why:** Clean, modern, excellent screen readability, supports Greek characters

## Why Katsoulidis is Perfect for This Project

1. **Greek Academic Heritage:** Designed as part of research programs in Greek TEI (Technical Educational Institutes)

2. **University Context:** Created specifically for Greek academic and cultural publications

3. **Designer Prestige:** Takis Katsoulidis is Greece's most renowned type designer, author of "The Design of the Letter"

4. **Didot Evolution:** Builds on the Didot tradition (which has deep historical connections to Greek printing) with modern improvements

5. **Bilingual Excellence:** Equal attention to both Greek and Latin alphabets

6. **Legibility:** Enhanced x-height and curves make it more readable than traditional Didot faces

7. **Cultural Authenticity:** Using Greek Font Society (Fonts.GR) typeface honors Greek typographic tradition

## About Takis Katsoulidis

Takis Katsoulidis is a Greek painter-engraver and type designer who has made fundamental contributions to modern Greek typography. His work is characterized by:

- Deep understanding of the unique forms of Greek letters (ζ, ξ, σ, ς, φ, ω, ψ)
- Creating typefaces that "breathe and vibrate"
- Blending traditional Greek forms with modern readability
- Numerous awards and exhibitions in Greece and internationally

His book "To Schedio tou Grammatos" (The Design of the Letter) is considered a seminal work in Greek typography education.

**CSS Variables:**
```css
--font-primary: 'Roboto', sans-serif;
--font-heading: 'Katsoulidis W01 Regular', Georgia, serif;
--font-accent: 'Katsoulidis W01 Regular', Georgia, serif;
```
