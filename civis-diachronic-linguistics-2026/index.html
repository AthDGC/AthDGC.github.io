Where Computation Meets Language History

Combining traditional philology with cutting-edge computational methods to study language evolution

Computational Historical Linguistics

This programme bridges traditional philological methods with computational techniques. Students engage with linguistic analysis through both theoretical and practical approaches.

The curriculum combines historical linguistics with digital methods to analyze language change across different periods.

Three-Phase Programme Structure

01

Spring School (Online)

April 20–24, 2026

Intensive week of online seminars covering theoretical foundations, computational methods, and project design with leading faculty from across Europe.

02

Online Workshops

May 2026

Collaborative online sessions on corpus analysis, statistical modeling, and machine learning applications. Work on individual "From Texts to Grammar" projects with faculty mentorship.

03

Summer School • Naxos

July 20–24, 2026

Present research findings on the stunning Cycladic island of Naxos. Five days of advanced seminars, project presentations, and cultural immersion in the heart of ancient Greek heritage.
