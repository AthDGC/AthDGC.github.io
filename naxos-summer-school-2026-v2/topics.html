Core Topics

Linguistic Diachronies: A Contrastive Approach

Main Themes

The Diachrony of English

Explore the evolution of English from Old English through Middle English to Modern English, including phonological, morphological, and syntactic changes over time.

  • Old English phonology and morphology
  • The Great Vowel Shift
  • Grammaticalization processes
  • Standardization and prescriptivism

New Perspectives in Studying Ancient and Medieval IE Languages

Examine innovative approaches to studying Indo-European languages, including corpus methods, digital humanities, and interdisciplinary perspectives.

  • Computational approaches to historical linguistics
  • Digital corpora and text analysis
  • Archaeological and genetic evidence
  • Reconstructing proto-languages

The Diachrony of Romance

Investigate the development of Romance languages from Latin, focusing on comparative analysis and language contact phenomena.

  • Vulgar Latin to Romance languages
  • Sound changes in Romance
  • Morphosyntactic evolution
  • Language contact and borrowing

Comparative and Contrastive Diachronic Linguistics

Learn methodologies for comparing language change across different language families and identifying universal patterns of linguistic evolution.

  • Comparative method
  • Language typology and change
  • Universals of language change
  • Case studies from diverse families

Language Contact

Study how languages influence each other through contact, including borrowing, code-switching, and the emergence of contact languages.

  • Types of language contact
  • Lexical borrowing
  • Grammatical borrowing
  • Pidgins and creoles

Workshop / Poster Session

Present your own research and receive feedback from international experts in diachronic linguistics.

  • Student presentations
  • Research methodology workshops
  • Data analysis techniques
  • Publication strategies

Learning Outcomes

Theoretical Knowledge

Master key theories and frameworks in diachronic linguistics

Methodological Skills

Apply comparative and computational methods to historical data

Research Experience

Develop and present original research in diachronic linguistics

International Network

Build connections with scholars and students worldwide
