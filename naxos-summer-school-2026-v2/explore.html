Explore Naxos

Discover the Beauty and History of the Island

Why Naxos?

Naxos is the largest island in the Cyclades, offering a perfect blend of stunning beaches, ancient history, traditional culture, and natural beauty. It's an ideal location for combining academic study with cultural exploration.

Historical Sites

Portara (Apollo's Gate)

The iconic marble gateway standing on Palatia islet, the remains of an unfinished temple dedicated to Apollo. Best viewed at sunset.

  • Symbol of Naxos
  • 524 BC construction
  • Spectacular sunset views
  • 5-minute walk from town

Ancient Naxos

Explore the remains of ancient Naxos city, including temples, fortifications, and early Christian churches.

  • Archaic and Classical periods
  • Temple of Dionysus
  • Early Christian basilicas
  • Archaeological museum nearby

Kastro (Medieval Castle)

The medieval Venetian castle in Naxos Town, built in 1207, offers stunning views and a journey through history.

  • 13th-century construction
  • Medieval architecture
  • Panoramic island views
  • Living museum

Beaches

Agios Georgios

Naxos Town

Long sandy beach perfect for swimming and water sports. Walking distance from town and venue.

  • Facilities: umbrellas, sunbeds
  • Water sports available
  • Beach bars and restaurants
  • Family-friendly

Plaka

South Coast

One of the longest beaches in Greece with golden sand and crystal-clear waters.

  • 6km of sandy beach
  • Partially organized
  • Secluded areas available
  • Bus access from town

Orkos

West Coast

Beautiful bay with excellent windsurfing conditions and stunning sunset views.

  • Windsurfing paradise
  • Sunset viewing spot
  • Taverna nearby
  • Less crowded

Mountain Villages

Apiranthos

Traditional mountain village known for its marble-paved streets, museums, and authentic Cycladic architecture.

  • Archaeological museum
  • Folk art museum
  • Traditional cafes
  • 30 minutes from town

Filoti

Charming village at the foot of Mount Zas, Greece's highest point in the Cyclades.

  • Base for hiking Mount Zas
  • Traditional architecture
  • Local tavernas
  • 25 minutes from town

Halki

Former capital of Naxos, known for its neoclassical architecture and citrus groves.

  • Valley of the Greeks
  • Kitron liqueur distillery
  • Historic churches
  • 20 minutes from town

Activities

Hiking

Explore ancient paths and mountain trails, including the hike to Mount Zas summit.

Water Sports

Windsurfing, kitesurfing, and sailing at various beaches around the island.

Cycling

Rent a bike to explore the countryside and coastal routes.

Boat Trips

Take day trips to nearby islands like Paros, Antiparos, and Delos.

Gastronomy

Naxos is famous for its local products including:

Naxian Cheese

Arseniko, graviera, and mizithra cheeses produced locally

Local Potatoes

Famous Naxian potatoes used in traditional dishes

Kitron Liqueur

Citrus liqueur produced in Halki since 1896

Olive Oil

Extra virgin olive oil from local groves

Best Time to Explore

July is perfect for beach activities and outdoor exploration. Early mornings and late afternoons are ideal for sightseeing to avoid the midday heat.

Getting Around

  • Local buses connect major villages and beaches
  • Taxi services available in Naxos Town
  • Car and scooter rentals for independent exploration
  • Walking is perfect for exploring Naxos Town
