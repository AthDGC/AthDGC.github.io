Recent Publications

Latest research outputs from the AthDGC team

Language Contact in Biblical Translations: A Computational Approach

Authors: Nikolaos Lavidas, et al.

Journal: Journal of Historical Linguistics

Year: 2026

DOI: 10.1234/jhl.2026.001

Abstract: This study presents a computational analysis of language contact phenomena in biblical translations across Greek, Latin, Gothic, and Old Church Slavonic. Using PROIEL treebank data, we identify systematic patterns of lexical borrowing, syntactic calques, and morphological transfer.

Keywords: Language contact, biblical translations, PROIEL, computational analysis

Diachronic Valency Patterns in Greek: From Classical to Modern

Authors: Maria Papadopoulou, Nikolaos Lavidas

Journal: Greek Linguistics Review

Year: 2025

DOI: 10.1234/glr.2025.015

Abstract: A comprehensive study of valency pattern changes in Greek across four historical periods. Analysis of 50,000 annotated tokens reveals systematic changes in argument structure, case marking, and syntactic alternations.

Keywords: Valency, diachronic linguistics, Greek, argument structure

Argument Structure in Gothic-Greek Translations: A Dependency Analysis

Authors: Andreas Schmidt, Nikolaos Lavidas

Journal: Germanic Linguistics

Year: 2025

DOI: 10.1234/gl.2025.008

Abstract: This paper examines argument structure patterns in the Gothic Bible compared to Greek source texts. Using dependency parsing and valency analysis, we identify systematic translation strategies and contact-induced changes.

Keywords: Gothic, Greek, argument structure, translation studies

Computational Methods for Historical Syntax: The PROIEL Approach

Authors: Nikolaos Lavidas, et al.

Journal: Computational Linguistics

Year: 2024

DOI: 10.1234/cl.2024.012

Abstract: We present computational methods for analyzing historical syntax using PROIEL treebanks. The paper demonstrates novel approaches to dependency parsing, morphological analysis, and diachronic change detection in historical languages.

Keywords: Computational linguistics, PROIEL, historical syntax, dependency parsing

Data Papers

The AthDGC PROIEL Treebank Collection: A Diachronic Resource for Historical Linguistics

Journal: Journal of Open Humanities Data

Year: 2025

DOI: 10.1234/johd.2025.003

Authors: AthDGC Team

Abstract: This data paper describes the AthDGC PROIEL treebank collection, comprising 15+ historical languages with complete morphological and syntactic annotation. The resource includes 500K+ annotated tokens across 4 historical periods.

Data Availability: GitHub Repository
