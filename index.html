About the Project

The Athens Digital Glossa Chronos (AthDGC) project is a comprehensive computational pipeline for analyzing verb valency patterns (argument structures) across historical periods, from Ancient Greek (8th c. BCE) to Modern English (21st c. CE). Using state-of-the-art NLP tools and automated linguistic annotation, we process 338 historical texts to track how verbs change their syntactic behavior over time.

The project develops corpus-based valency lexicons for ancient and medieval languages using innovative computational methods integrated with traditional philological expertise. Our research spans 2,800 years across 8 historical periods, from Archaic Greek to Modern English.

Funded by the Hellenic Foundation for Research and Innovation (HFRI) at the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens. Principal Investigator: Prof. Nikolaos Lavidas.
