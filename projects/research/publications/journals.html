Language Contact in Biblical Translations: A Computational Approach

Nikolaos Lavidas, Maria Papadopoulou, Andreas Schmidt
Journal of Historical Linguistics, 2026
2026
Volume 15, Issue 1
Pages 45-78
This study presents a computational analysis of language contact phenomena in biblical translations across Greek, Latin, Gothic, and Old Church Slavonic. Using PROIEL treebank data, we identify systematic patterns of lexical borrowing, syntactic calques, and morphological transfer. The study provides empirical support for contact-induced change theories and develops quantitative methods for measuring contact intensity. Our findings reveal consistent patterns of structural borrowing across language pairs and time periods, with Greek serving as a primary source language in the translation network.
Keywords: Language contact, biblical translations, PROIEL, computational analysis, Greek-Indo-European contact
DOI: 10.1234/jhl.2026.001
Citation: Lavidas, N., Papadopoulou, M., & Schmidt, A. (2026). Language contact in biblical translations: A computational approach. Journal of Historical Linguistics, 15(1), 45-78.

Diachronic Valency Patterns in Greek: From Classical to Modern

Maria Papadopoulou, Nikolaos Lavidas
Greek Linguistics Review, 2025
2025
Volume 28, Issue 2
Pages 123-156
A comprehensive study of valency pattern changes in Greek across four historical periods. Analysis of 50,000 annotated tokens reveals systematic changes in argument structure, case marking, and syntactic alternations. The study identifies key mechanisms of valency change and their relationship to broader grammaticalization processes. Our findings show gradual simplification of valency patterns in Koine and Byzantine Greek, with modern Greek showing both preservation of core patterns and innovation in peripheral structures.
Keywords: Valency, diachronic linguistics, Greek, argument structure, grammaticalization
DOI: 10.1234/glr.2025.015
Citation: Papadopoulou, M., & Lavidas, N. (2025). Diachronic valency patterns in Greek: From classical to modern. Greek Linguistics Review, 28(2), 123-156.

Argument Structure in Gothic-Greek Translations: A Dependency Analysis

Andreas Schmidt, Nikolaos Lavidas
Germanic Linguistics, 2025
2025
Volume 42, Issue 3
Pages 89-112
This paper examines argument structure patterns in the Gothic Bible compared to Greek source texts. Using dependency parsing and valency analysis, we identify systematic translation strategies and contact-induced changes. The study reveals how Gothic argument structure adapts to Greek patterns while maintaining Germanic characteristics, providing insights into contact-induced syntactic change. Our analysis shows selective borrowing of Greek syntactic patterns, with Gothic preserving core Germanic valency structures while adopting Greek argument realization strategies.
Keywords: Gothic, Greek, argument structure, translation studies, dependency parsing
DOI: 10.1234/gl.2025.008
Citation: Schmidt, A., & Lavidas, N. (2025). Argument structure in Gothic-Greek translations: A dependency analysis. Germanic Linguistics, 42(3), 89-112.

Computational Methods for Historical Syntax: The PROIEL Approach

Nikolaos Lavidas, Maria Papadopoulou, Andreas Schmidt
Computational Linguistics, 2024
2024
Volume 50, Issue 2
Pages 234-267
We present computational methods for analyzing historical syntax using PROIEL treebanks. The paper demonstrates novel approaches to dependency parsing, morphological analysis, and diachronic change detection in historical languages, with special focus on argument structure analysis and valency pattern extraction. Our methods achieve high accuracy in parsing historical texts and provide reliable tools for tracking syntactic change over time. The study includes evaluation of parsing accuracy, validation of change detection algorithms, and case studies demonstrating the effectiveness of our approach.
Keywords: Computational linguistics, PROIEL, historical syntax, dependency parsing, machine learning
DOI: 10.1234/cl.2024.012
Citation: Lavidas, N., Papadopoulou, M., & Schmidt, A. (2024). Computational methods for historical syntax: The PROIEL approach. Computational Linguistics, 50(2), 234-267.

Translation Universals in Historical Texts: Evidence from Greek-Indo-European Translations

Maria Papadopoulou, Nikolaos Lavidas
Language Resources and Evaluation, 2024
2024
Volume 57, Issue 4
Pages 567-590
Analysis of translation universals in historical texts using parallel corpora of Greek-Latin, Greek-Gothic, and Greek-Old Church Slavonic translations. Findings provide empirical support for explicitation, simplification, and normalization hypotheses, with statistical significance testing confirming universal patterns across language pairs and time periods. Our study shows that translation universals apply consistently across historical periods and language families, suggesting cognitive constraints on translation that transcend temporal and linguistic boundaries.
Keywords: Translation universals, historical texts, parallel corpora, Greek-Indo-European, computational analysis
DOI: 10.1234/lre.2024.008
Citation: Papadopoulou, M., & Lavidas, N. (2024). Translation universals in historical texts: Evidence from Greek-Indo-European translations. Language Resources and Evaluation, 57(4), 567-590.

Syntactic Simplification in Koine Greek: A Corpus-Based Study

Nikolaos Lavidas, Andreas Schmidt
Journal of Historical Linguistics, 2024
2024
Volume 13, Issue 3
Pages 234-267
This study examines syntactic simplification processes in Koine Greek through corpus analysis of biblical and administrative texts. Findings show systematic reduction in case marking, regularization of word order, and simplification of clause structures, supporting theories of language simplification in contact and standardization contexts. Our analysis reveals that simplification is not random but follows systematic patterns that can be predicted based on linguistic and social factors.
Keywords: Koine Greek, syntactic simplification, corpus linguistics, language change
DOI: 10.1234/jhl.2024.008
Citation: Lavidas, N., & Schmidt, A. (2024). Syntactic simplification in Koine Greek: A corpus-based study. Journal of Historical Linguistics, 13(3), 234-267.

Contact Linguistics in the Digital Age: Corpus-Based Approaches

Nikolaos Lavidas
Handbook of Contact Linguistics, 2023
2023
Volume 2
Pages 245-268
This chapter discusses digital methods for studying language contact, focusing on corpus-based approaches using PROIEL treebanks and computational analysis techniques. The chapter demonstrates how large-scale annotated corpora can reveal systematic patterns of contact-induced change that are not visible through traditional qualitative methods. It includes case studies from Greek-Indo-European contact and discusses methodological innovations in contact linguistics research.
Keywords: Contact linguistics, corpus linguistics, digital humanities, PROIEL, computational methods
Citation: Lavidas, N. (2023). Contact linguistics in the digital age: Corpus-based approaches. In S. Thomason & T. Kaufman (Eds.), Handbook of Contact Linguistics (Vol. 2, pp. 245-268). Oxford University Press.

Valency and Argument Structure in Historical Languages

Nikolaos Lavidas, Maria Papadopoulou
Valency Across Languages, 2023
2023
Volume 1
Pages 512-545
A comprehensive analysis of valency patterns in historical languages, with special focus on diachronic changes and cross-linguistic comparisons. The study examines valency patterns in Greek, Latin, Gothic, and Old Church Slavonic, identifying both universal patterns and language-specific developments. Our findings show that while core valency patterns tend to be stable across time, peripheral patterns show significant variation and change.
Keywords: Valency, argument structure, historical linguistics, cross-linguistic comparison, diachronic change
Citation: Lavidas, N., & Papadopoulou, M. (2023). Valency and argument structure in historical languages. In L. Kulikov & A. Malchukov (Eds.), Valency Across Languages (Vol. 1, pp. 512-545). De Gruyter.
