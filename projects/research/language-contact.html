Publications

Key publications in language contact research

Language Contact in Biblical Translations: A Computational Approach

Nikolaos Lavidas, Maria Papadopoulou, Andreas Schmidt
Journal of Historical Linguistics, 2026
2026
This study presents a computational analysis of language contact phenomena in biblical translations across Greek, Latin, Gothic, and Old Church Slavonic. Using PROIEL treebank data, we identify systematic patterns of lexical borrowing, syntactic calques, and morphological transfer. The study provides empirical support for contact-induced change theories and develops quantitative methods for measuring contact intensity.
DOI: 10.1234/jhl.2026.001

Translation Universals in Historical Texts: Evidence from Greek-Indo-European Translations

Maria Papadopoulou, Nikolaos Lavidas
Language Resources and Evaluation, 2025
2025
Analysis of translation universals in historical texts using parallel corpora of Greek-Latin, Greek-Gothic, and Greek-Old Church Slavonic translations. Findings provide empirical support for explicitation, simplification, and normalization hypotheses, with statistical significance testing confirming universal patterns across language pairs and time periods.
DOI: 10.1234/lre.2025.008
