Key Research Areas

Major domains of historical linguistic investigation

Grammaticalization

Study of grammatical changes and their mechanisms

  • Lexical to grammatical shift
  • Morphosyntactic reanalysis
  • Phonological reduction
  • Semantic bleaching
  • Pathways of change

Methodology

Tracking grammaticalization pathways through corpus analysis, identifying intermediate stages, and modeling the directionality and speed of change.

Syntactic Change

Analysis of syntactic structure evolution over time

  • Word order changes
  • Clause structure evolution
  • Syntactic category shifts
  • Embedding patterns
  • Coordination development

Methodology

Dependency parsing of historical texts, statistical analysis of syntactic patterns, and comparative study of syntactic typology across periods.

Morphological Evolution

Study of morphological system changes and innovations

  • Inflectional paradigm changes
  • Case system evolution
  • Derivational morphology
  • Paradigm leveling
  • Morphological innovation

Methodology

Systematic analysis of morphological annotation in treebanks, tracking paradigm changes, and modeling morphological productivity over time.

Lexical Change

Analysis of vocabulary development and semantic shift

  • Lexical borrowing patterns
  • Semantic shift mechanisms
  • Word formation processes
  • Lexical replacement
  • Specialization trends

Methodology

Frequency analysis of lexical items, semantic field mapping, and computational modeling of lexical change patterns.

Publications

Key publications in historical linguistics

Diachronic Valency Patterns in Greek: From Classical to Modern

Maria Papadopoulou, Nikolaos Lavidas
Greek Linguistics Review, 2025
2025
A comprehensive study of valency pattern changes in Greek across four historical periods. Analysis of 50,000 annotated tokens reveals systematic changes in argument structure, case marking, and syntactic alternations. The study identifies key mechanisms of valency change and their relationship to broader grammaticalization processes.
DOI: 10.1234/glr.2025.015

Syntactic Simplification in Koine Greek: A Corpus-Based Study

Nikolaos Lavidas, Andreas Schmidt
Journal of Historical Linguistics, 2024
2024
This study examines syntactic simplification processes in Koine Greek through corpus analysis of biblical and administrative texts. Findings show systematic reduction in case marking, regularization of word order, and simplification of clause structures, supporting theories of language simplification in contact and standardization contexts.
DOI: 10.1234/jhl.2024.008
